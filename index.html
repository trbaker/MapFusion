Basemap

Pick the background map your map sits on. Imagery shows photos from space; Topographic shows roads and land features.

Search ArcGIS Online for free public layers — try “rivers”, “earthquakes”, “national parks”, or “population”. Then press Add.

Turn layers on and off, reorder them, or remove ones you don’t need.

Measure distance along a line or the area of a shape. Click on the map to place points; double-click to finish.

Draw points, lines, and shapes right on the map. Use the tools below to add, move, and delete your drawings.

See latitude and longitude. Move your mouse over the map to read coordinates, click the map to lock one in, or type coordinates to jump there.

A quick tour of every tool. GIS stands for Geographic Information System — software for making and studying maps.

🗺️Basemap — change the background map (satellite, streets, topographic…).
➕Add data — find free public map layers and add them on top.
📋Layers — show, hide, reorder, or remove your layers.
📏Measure — find how long or how big something is.
✏️Sketch — draw your own points, lines, and shapes.
📍Coords — read and enter latitude/longitude.
🔍Search (top of map) — find a place or address.
💾Save / Open — save your map as a file, then open it again later. The file follows the ArcGIS web map format, so it also works in ArcGIS Online.

ArcGIS API key (optional)

The app works without a key. Adding a free key from ArcGIS Location Platform turns on Esri place search and extra basemaps. Your key is stored only on this computer.
